If there is no good seed given, use a good random seed

  1. We will generate a sparse matrix in the following way:
    • All the diagonal entries are non-zero — this accounts for n nnz.
    • Since we are dealing with symmetric matrices, exactly half of these entries should be in the UPPER triangle and then we can simply reflect everything about the diagonal. I.e., we need to figure out where to place the remaining (nnz-n)/2 entries. There are (n*n-n)/2 possible slots for these in the UPPER triangle.

    • Go through each element in the UPPER triangle and choose to keep it with a probability of (nnz-n)/(n*n-n).

      Caveat: All this assumes the following: (nnz>n and nnz<(n*n))
